HINEYCAST

Precipitation Accumulation Tracker

What is this? Click to learn more

Hineycast is a tool for comparing the current year's precipitation accumulation against historical norms. It pulls high-quality spatial climate data from the PRISM Climate Group to compute a 30-year daily pseudo-normal (1991-2020) and plots it against the observed precipitation accumulation for the current year.

Whether you are a farmer, gardener, or just curious about the climate, Hineycast helps you easily visualize whether your location is experiencing a surplus or deficit of precipitation compared to what is historically expected.

Please note that PRISM data covers only the contiguous United States and is typically updated with provisional data daily, though data for the most recent days may occasionally experience short delays.
